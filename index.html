Project

ClimaGuard

A novel, open-source framework that bridges the gap between high-fidelity climate modelling and computational feasibility for solar-radiation-management governance and research.

As the impacts of global warming intensify, solar radiation management (SRM) — including stratospheric aerosol injection (SAI) and marine cloud brightening (MCB) — is increasingly scrutinised as a potential cooling strategy. The use of these techniques is hampered by the immense computational cost of traditional numerical climate models, which limits our ability to quantify regional uncertainties and extreme-weather risks.

By integrating Deep Learning with Explainable AI, ClimaGuard provides accelerated, high-resolution predictions while maintaining the physical consistency required for trustworthy policy and scientific research.

Core methodology

The architecture is built upon a hybrid atmospheric modelling approach that combines a differentiable dynamical core with neural physics modules.

Hybrid modelling
A probabilistic model that processes high-dimensional climate state variables, stochastic noise and explicit forcing parameters — rapid simulations without sacrificing the rigor of traditional physics.
Data & training
Trained on comprehensive datasets from CMIP6 ARISE-SAI and GeoMIP, covering a variety of MCB and SAI scenarios. Historical volcanic eruptions serve as natural analogs to validate predictive skill on aerosol forcing.
Optimisation strategy
A multi-level adaptation strategy: fine-tuning ML components against high-resolution benchmarks (e.g. ERA5) and, where necessary, extending the physical core to better capture aerosol transport and stratospheric circulation.

Risk assessment & interpretability

Beyond simple temperature modelling, ClimaGuard focuses on the tail risks of climate intervention.

Extreme events
Frequency and intensity of tropical cyclones, precipitation shifts and temperature extremes.
Counterfactual analysis
Researchers can explore "what-if" scenarios — e.g. the regional impact of adjusting aerosol injection altitudes.
Transparency (XAI)
Mechanistic interpretability ensures predictions adhere to known physical laws and provides attribution mapping to identify the specific drivers of regional climate risks.

Impact

By overcoming the computational bottlenecks of standard climate models, ClimaGuard provides an agile, high-fidelity toolbox. It empowers scientists and policymakers to explore the localised and global consequences of SRM with unprecedented speed and transparency.

Tools

Webtool

We aim to deploy ClimaGuard as a webtool providing fair and equal access to climate intervention insights. The webtool will allow users to explore their own intervention scenarios by uploading the inital conditions as data files. In a later version, we also plan to provide a user-friendly LLM interface for adjusting the intervention parameters directly on the webtool, without needing to upload data files.
